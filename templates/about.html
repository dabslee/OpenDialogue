    Mission

    OpenDiscussion is a forum website designed to promote safe, high-quality discussions between opposing ideas.

    Online spaces often struggle with three interlacing problems:

    1. Echo chambers: Online spaces often degenerate into echo chambers, where people end up only exposed to ideas that they agree with, radicalizing them. Sometimes, censorship can aid in this, as censorship might asymmetrically reduce exposure to certain opinions.
    2. Safety and quality: On the other end of the spectrum, a complete lack of censorship or moderation can lead to unsafe discussions or poor-quality posting.

    OpenDiscussion's purpose is to try and provide an online forum that addresses these issues--to provide a forum that is safe and high-quality without resorting to censorship.

    Highlighted Features

    • Posts: Users can write and upload posts to our forum. Posts have a minimum length of 2000 characters (~300 words) in order to promote more thought-out posts.
    • Upvote system: Instead of a generic upvote/downvote system, we have specific agreement & strength voting. Users vote individually on whether they agree with a post and whether they think a post makes a strong argument.
    • Responding to posts: Instead of comments, you respond to posts with your own post. Thus, instead of brief superficial comments, to respond to a post you are encouraged to make your own well-thought-out argument.
    • Recommended posts feed: The landing page for the user is a recommended posts feed, where you receive 5 posts daily that OpenDiscussion recommends reading. The site prioritizes high strength, high controversiality posts.
